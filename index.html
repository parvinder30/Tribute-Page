Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

"The People's President"


Here's a time line of Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam life:

  • 1931: - Born Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam on October 15, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1954: - Graduates in Physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, in 1954.
  • 1960: - Gains degree in Aeronautical Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology and joins the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) as scientist.
  • 1969: - Kalam moves to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he helms India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III)
  • 1970: - Kalam heads Project Devil and Project Valiant, which developed ballistic missiles from the technology of the SLV programme.
  • 1980: - Kalam leads India’s efforts to enter the space club by putting the Rohini satellite in Earth's orbit with the first indigenous SLV-III.
  • 1980: - As the chief of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme, he is credited with the development and operationalisation of Agni and Prithvi missiles.
  • 1981: - Kalam recieves the Padma Bhushan for his contribution to the field of science and technology.
  • 1990: - Kalam is awarded the Padma Vibhushan, India’s second highest civilian honour.
  • 1992: - Kalam is appointed as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and the Secretary of the DRDO.
  • 1997: - Kalam is awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honour for his contribution to the scientific research and modernisation of defence technology in India.
  • 1998: - India conducts the Pokhran II nuclear tests with Kalam being one of the forces behind its success
  • 1999: - Serves as Principal Scientific Advisor to the Government of India
  • 2002: - Kalam is elected the 11th President of India succeeding KR Narayanan. He won the 2002 presidential election with an electoral vote of 922,884. During his five years in office, he was fondly called ‘The People’s President’.
  • 2012: - Kalam launches the ‘What Can I Give Movement’, a programme for the Indian youth aimed at defeating corruption.
  • 2014: On May 17, 2014, he resigned from his post of Prime Minister of India
  • 2015: - Kalam passes away on 27 July 2015 aged 83, after collapsing because of a cardiac arrest while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong
